Using Soundtrack

The Soundtrack window appears when you open Soundtrack (either by clicking its icon in Finder or in your Dock).

Click on the play button in the middle of the Soundtrack window. This button is shaped like the Soundtrack icon. You will see an activity indicator as Soundtrack tunes into the radio station.

Once the music starts playing, the title of the currently playing song will appear in the Soundtrack window.

To pause the music, click anywhere in the Soundtrack window.

You can move the Soundtrack window around in your screen by clicking and dragging anywhere inside the window. You can resize the Soundtrack window by dragging the edges.

Click the "zoom" button (the green button in the button set at the top left of the Soundtrack window) to make Soundtrack full screen; clicking it again will make Soundtrack into a window again.

Option-clicking the zoom button will toggle between the default size and any resizing that you've done.

Click the "close" button (the red button in the button set at the top left of the Soundtrack window) to hide the Soundtrack window. To make it visible again, click on the Soundtrack icon in the Dock.

Soundtrack adds an icon to the "Menu Extras" / "Status Bar" area at the top right of your screen. You can click this icon to play or pause Soundtrack.

The icon will appear dimmed if Soundtrack is not currently playing any music.

When Soundtrack is playing music, then you can see the title of the currently playing song as the tooltip of this status bar icon.

If you wish, you can disable this status bar icon by using the "Status Bar Icon" toggle item in the "View" menu.

When the Soundtrack window is not visible, Soundtrack will slide in a notification from the right of the screen when the song changes. If you wish, you can disable these notification by using the "Song Change Notifications" toggle item in the "View" menu.

There are keyboard shortcuts for some common tasks:

Space    Play/Pause
Command-H    Hide the Soundtrack window

 

About Soundtrack

To know more about Soundtrack, you can visit its website at https://github.com/mnvr/Soundtrack.

Soundtrack plays music from the DroneZone radio station hosted by Soma.FM. To know more about the music, you can visit their website at https://SomaFM.com/dronezone/.
